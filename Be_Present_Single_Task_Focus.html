Be Present: Single Task Focus

Focus is built by returning.

Your attention will wander. You may get distracted, pulled into thoughts, irritated with yourself, or emotionally hooked.

The skill is not perfect focus. The skill is noticing you left and coming back without turning it into a shame spiral.

Be Present means: every time you notice you are distracted, that moment becomes the practice.

Pick one focus target.

Choose the situation where you want to practice bringing your focus back to one thing.

Choose a simple label.

External distractions
  • Phone
  • Noise
  • People
  • Notifications
  • Movement
Internal distractions
  • Worrying
  • Planning
  • Remembering
  • Judging
  • Daydreaming

Use the lightest possible label: “phone,” “noise,” “worrying,” “planning.” Then return to the task without analyzing the distraction.

Set a short practice.

You are not trying to win at focus. You are practicing the return.
