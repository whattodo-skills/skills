🧊 Cool Down with Cold Water
1

Fill a bowl or sink with cold water — or grab a few ice cubes.

2

Take a slow, full breath in through your nose.

3

Hold your breath and submerge your face in the water — or hold ice against your wrists or cheeks. Stay for 30 seconds.

30
4

Lift your face (or remove the ice). Breathe out slowly through your mouth.

5

Notice how your body feels. Your heart rate has slowed. Your nervous system is calmer.

Cold water activates the dive reflex — a built-in physiological response that rapidly slows your heart rate and quiets your nervous system. It works within seconds.

🕯️ Soothe Yourself Through the Senses
👁️ SIGHT ▾
  • Look at something beautiful — a photo, plants, the sky outside.
  • Watch a candle flame flicker, or find a video of flowing water.
  • Step outside and let your eyes rest on the horizon.
🎵 SOUND ▾
  • Play soft music or nature sounds — rain, ocean, forest.
  • Listen to a song that feels like a warm hug.
  • Sit in silence and just notice the sounds around you.
🌸 SMELL ▾
  • Smell something comforting — lotion, coffee, a candle, a favorite soap.
  • Step outside and breathe in fresh air.
  • Brew tea or coffee just to smell it warm in your hands.
☕ TASTE ▾
  • Make a warm drink — tea, coffee, hot cocoa — and sip it slowly.
  • Eat one small piece of chocolate. Let it melt. Be fully there for it.
  • Drink a cold glass of water and notice every sensation.
🤲 TOUCH ▾
  • Wrap yourself in a soft blanket or cozy sweater.
  • Hold something warm (a mug) or something cool (a smooth stone, ice).
  • Pet an animal. Or give yourself a slow, gentle hand massage.
