Describe: Say What Is Happening

Describe means putting words on what you noticed.

This practice helps you name thoughts, feelings, body sensations, urges, assumptions, and facts without adding judgment or a story that pulls you into reacting.

Knowing what is happening inside matters because thoughts, emotions, body signals, urges, and assumptions can quietly steer your behavior. When you can describe them clearly, you have more room to choose what to do next.

From observe to describe

Observe is “notice what is happening.” Describe is “put words on what you noticed.” The goal is clear wording, not perfect wording.

Try this phrase: “I notice…” or “I’m having the thought that…” or “I have an urge to…”

Describe without adding a story

A fact is observable. An interpretation or assumption is the meaning your mind adds. Describing helps you slow down before reacting from assumptions.

Example: Instead of “She hates me,” describe: “She did not text me back, and I am assuming she is upset with me.”
