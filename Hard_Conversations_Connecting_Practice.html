Hard Conversations Practice

Hard conversations need three things.

Clarity so you know what you are asking for. Steadiness so you do not get pulled off track. Respect so you can protect the relationship and not abandon yourself.

This tool helps you plan what to say when you need to ask for something, say no, set a limit, or stay honest without blowing things up.

The aim: say the thing clearly, stay connected where possible, and keep your self-respect intact.

Clarify your main goal.

If you do not know your priority, the conversation can pull you everywhere. Choose what matters most this time.

Choose your conversation stance.

These three reminders work together. Pick the one you most need to keep in front of you.

Say it clearly

Describe the facts, say how it affects you, ask for what you need, and explain why it matters.

Stay connected

Be gentle, show interest, validate what you can, and keep your manner steady.

Stay true to yourself

Be fair, avoid unnecessary apologies, stick to your values, and tell the truth simply.

Your earlier choice will shape your script, but all three parts matter.
