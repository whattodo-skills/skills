Observe: Notice Before You React

Observe means noticing what is happening before you react.

This practice helps you watch thoughts, feelings, body sensations, urges, and your surroundings without immediately judging, explaining, fixing, avoiding, or acting on them.

What observing is

Observing is simply noticing the experience. You are not trying to stop a thought, hold onto a feeling, solve the urge, or force your body to change.

The skill: let thoughts, feelings, sensations, urges, sounds, sights, and memories enter and leave awareness.

Why it helps

When you observe, you create space between what happens and what you do next. That space gives you a better chance to respond on purpose.

Remember: you are controlling your attention, not controlling the whole experience.
