Pause Before Acting

A pause is a brief space between an impulse and your response.

When something happens with a person or situation, your body and mind may react quickly. This practice helps you notice that immediate reaction, label it as an emotion, and choose what is effective instead of reacting automatically.

What the pause is

A pause is not doing nothing forever. It is a short moment where you stop, breathe, and notice the impulse before you speak, send, leave, argue, shut down, explain, fix, or avoid.

The skill: name the emotion underneath the reaction, then choose your next move on purpose.

Why it matters

Immediate reactions can feel urgent, especially when you feel criticized, disappointed, embarrassed, pressured, ignored, threatened, or overwhelmed. Labeling the emotion gives your brain a handle for what is happening.

Benefit: the pause helps you protect relationships, reduce regret, and respond in a way that fits your values and the moment.

Label the emotion

Choose the emotion that best names your immediate reaction. Keep the label simple. You are not proving it, defending it, or judging it.

Name the moment

Use one real situation or person. The goal is to catch the first impulse before it runs the show.
