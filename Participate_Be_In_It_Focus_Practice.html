Participate: Be In It While You’re Doing It

Participate means fully entering the moment.

This practice helps you move out of overthinking, self-monitoring, hesitation, and half-attention — and back into direct engagement with the task, conversation, or experience in front of you.

Stop watching yourself from the outside

Participating means joining the moment instead of standing outside of it, judging how you are doing, rehearsing what comes next, or checking out.

The skill: throw yourself into the activity with your full attention, then come back when your mind drifts.

Everyday moments count

Walking, cleaning, listening, working, playing, cooking, singing, talking, and hobbies can all become Focus practice when you do them fully.

Reminder: you do not have to do it perfectly. Participating means returning to the experience again and again.
