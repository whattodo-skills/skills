Ride the Wave Practice

An urge is a wave, not a command.

When an urge gets strong, it can feel like you have to do something right now: send the text, keep arguing, slam the door, keep scrolling, buy the thing, say the sharp thing.

This practice helps you ride out the peak without making the situation worse. You will name the urge, track it, let it rise and fall, and choose your next move from a steadier place.

The goal: delay the reaction long enough for the wave to shift. You do not have to love the feeling. You only have to stay with it without handing it the steering wheel.

Name the urge.

Getting specific helps separate you from the pull. Instead of “I am out of control,” try “I am noticing the urge to react.”
