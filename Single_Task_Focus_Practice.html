Managing Distractions: Single Task Focus

The One Task Start When You're Distracted

Big, vague tasks make it hard for your brain to know where to begin. When the task feels unclear or overwhelming, focus often looks for something easier, faster, or more interesting.

Managing Distractions: Single Task Focus reduces the task to one visible, physical action so your mind has a clear place to return.

What To Do:

Choose one small physical action. Expect your attention to move. When it does, name what pulled it away, take one breath, and put your hands or eyes back on the action you chose.

You are not trying to hold perfect focus. You are practicing the return.

Each noticed drift and intentional return is a practice repetition.

The skill-building loop: learn the method, plan a small action, practice with a timer, measure returns, reflect, adjust, apply it to real life, and repeat.
