Stone Focus Practice

Give your attention somewhere steadier.

When your mind keeps circling the same thought, your attention can start to feel hijacked. This practice gives your mind one simple job: follow a small stone as it slowly sinks through clear water.

The point is not to force every thought away. The point is to notice when your focus wanders, then gently place it back on the image.

Use this when: you catch yourself scrolling without meaning to, replaying a conversation, checking headlines over and over, or feeding a thought that is not helping you.

First, name what keeps pulling you.

Pick the closest option. This is not about judging yourself. It is just a way to notice where your attention has been going.

Your only job: follow the stone. When your mind wanders, notice it and return to the stone.
