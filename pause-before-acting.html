Pause Before Acting

Recognize your pause cue, create space between impulse and response, then choose what is effective.

A pause is a brief space between an impulse and your response.

Pause Before Acting is a Focus rehearsal. It trains you to notice the precise cue that says, "I need a pause," then shift attention from impulse to choice. The STOP Skill is for broader emergency coping; this skill is for recognizing your personal interruption cue before the next reaction takes over.

Safety first: Use this pause when it is safe to wait briefly. When you or someone else is in immediate danger, prioritize safety and get appropriate help rather than delaying action to complete the exercise.

What the pause does

A pause is not ignoring what you feel. Your emotion is valid. The pause helps you decide how you want to express it, instead of letting the strongest impulse decide for you.

The sequence: Stop. Breathe in through your nose. Breathe out through your mouth like blowing through a straw. Name the emotion. Notice the impulse. Choose what is effective.

What effective means

An effective response is safe, consistent with what matters to you, likely to help the situation rather than only release the impulse, and respectful of your needs and reasonable boundaries.

Benefit: The pause does not force the feeling to disappear. It gives you room to choose behavior with less regret.
