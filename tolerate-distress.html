Coping

Get Through a Hard Moment

Feel what is here without making the next moment harder.

A hard feeling is not a command.

A hard moment can make your mind demand relief right now. You may feel pulled to react, escape, argue, send a message, shut down, numb out, or make a fast choice.

This practice does not mean approving of the situation or staying in danger. It means staying with the feeling long enough to choose what helps instead of automatically doing what the pull demands.

Success is not feeling calm immediately. Success is noticing the pull to react, creating a pause, and avoiding an action that would make the next moment harder.
Safety first: Do not use this exercise to delay protective action. When you or someone else is in immediate danger, prioritize safety and appropriate help.
You do not have to like this feeling, solve everything, or follow the pull. You only need to get through the next moment safely.
