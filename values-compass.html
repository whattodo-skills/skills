Choose What Matters: The Values Compass

Values help you decide where your focus belongs. This practice turns a value into one small action so you can return to what matters when stress, urgency, or avoidance pulls your attention away.

What is a value?

A value is a continuous direction, not a destination. It names the quality of action you want to practice. Goals tell you what you hope will happen. Values tell you how you want to show up while you take the next step.

Example: If you value Reliability, your next action might be opening the difficult email and writing one honest sentence. If you value Kindness, your next action might be pausing before a hard conversation and asking, "What is the kind response here?"

When life gets loud, your focus can get pulled toward urgency, avoidance, worry, or other people's reactions. Your values give your attention a place to return.

The focus skill: pause, ask "What matters here?", choose one value, and put your next small action where that value points.

Values vs. Goals

It is easy to confuse a value with a goal. A goal is "finish the project by Friday." A value is "diligence" or "excellence." If you hit a roadblock, your attention may jump to failure, shame, or panic, but you can still return your focus to diligence by communicating clearly and taking the next steady step.

Goals

  • External outcomes
  • Can be blocked by circumstances
  • You either hit them or you do not
  • "Finish the project by Friday"

Values

  • Internal directions
  • Always available in your next action
  • You can return to them after drifting
  • "I act with diligence"
Daily alignment check

At the end of the day, ask: "Where did I give my attention to what matters?" and "Where did I drift?" This is not self-criticism. It is information you can use to choose tomorrow's next step.
